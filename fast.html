Everybody Hurts

A brief note about our corporate point of view regarding the people we coach. After I had won the 1990 Ironman I upheld the tradition where the winner comes back to the finish line to cheer in the final finishers. As luck would have it, standing next me that night was Scott Tinley who has never lacked for a unique point of view. He turned to me just before the final finisher arrived after having been on the course for 17 hours, and said; "I could never race for 17 hours, these people humble me every year." That stuck with me and as we enter our 2.0 phase our point of view is that there are only two things that separate me going 8:09 and one of our team going 16:59 at an Ironman distance race, or any race for that matter. The first is genetics, most of the people you see standing on podiums should be very thankful that they were awarded a genetic makeup that made them faster than everyone they started with almost immediately. The second is that my coaches and I have raced a combined total of almost 800 triathlons at every distance, across every terrain, so the difference is experience. We can't give you genetics, but we can give you the benefit of our experience, and believe me every triathlete who finishes their race hurts every bit as bad as we did, you guys just endure it longer.
